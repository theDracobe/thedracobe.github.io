Class Projects

This page details many of the class projects I completed throughout my time at the Univerity of Pittsburgh. These represent some of the best learning oportunities I had at Pitt, providing a space to fully experience the topics I had learned.

MEMS 24 - Intro To Mechanical Design

Intro to mech design is a project based class designed to introduce and develop the concept and practice of engineering design process. The two main projects completed were the Solar Powered Rover and the Big Machine.

Solar Powered Rover

This the first physical project I had and remains one of my favorites. The rover project introduced me to the Makerspace and made extensive use of the laser cutter. Named as fabrication lead, I was responsible for using SolidWorks and the Makerspace tools to manufacture the components of each rover revision. I also learned about gear train design throughout this project. The goal of the rover was to compete in a speed test while holding a 100g mass and then complete an obstacle course.

Big Machine

The second project emphasized developing communication skills between teams. The goal was to create a Rube Goldberg machine spanning over 3 floors of the engineering building. With limited approved materials and spacial constriants (doors, corners, stairs) this project was very creative. I led the ideation process and the creation of the pendulum across the door. I also created a SolidWorks animation of the final design.

ENGR 0135 - Statics and Mechanics of Materials 1

Balsa Wood Bridge Analysis and Testing

The class project for Statics 1 was to research a truss bridge design, digitally design and analyze all structural stresses, and then construct and test a balsa wood model to failure. I later became an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for the class and project.

MEMS 1041 - Mechanical Measurements 1

Throughout the semester we completed testing on the failure of a replacement glass iPhone screen.
